## Abstract
This paper introduces the Adinkra-Stabilized Hypercube Model (ASH Model), a theoretical framework embedding supersymmetric adinkra graphs and Lindenmayer Systems (L-Systems) within a 9-dimensional hypercube structure. Grounded in the mathematical properties of 9—such as its refactorability (\( \tau(9) = 3 \), with 9 mod 3 = 0) and role in modular arithmetic—we formalize connections to supersymmetry (SUSY) representations via Sylvester James Gates' adinkras, string theory's critical dimension derivation (D=10, implying 9 spatial), and Hugh Everett's Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) of quantum mechanics. Through explicit derivations and computational simulations, we demonstrate emergent properties such as bell-curve realm distributions, self-correcting error-code behavior, and fractal branching patterns that mimic quantum decoherence. The model posits 9 as a stabilizing dimensional factor for theoretical consistency, with potential applications in quantum visualization, educational simulations, and interdisciplinary unification frameworks. Proof-of-concept results and limitations are discussed.

**Keywords**: Supersymmetry, Adinkras, String Theory, Many-Worlds Interpretation, L-Systems, Procedural Generation, 9 Dimensions, Error-Correcting Codes
