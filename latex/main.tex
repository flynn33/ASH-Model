\begin{abstract}
The Adinkra-Stabilized Hypercube Model (ASH Model) proposes a procedural cosmological framework constructed on a 9-dimensional hypercube whose vertices represent distinct realms. Supersymmetric adinkra graphs and doubly-even self-dual error-correcting codes are embedded at each vertex, providing algebraic structure that enforces symmetry transformations and robust error correction.

Agent-based simulations demonstrate rapid convergence to Gaussian occupancy distributions across realms, resilience to random bit-flip noise up to the theoretical Hamming bound, and fractal branching patterns generated by Lindenmayer systems that visualize quantum decoherence in the Many-Worlds Interpretation.

The recurrence of nine dimensions is mathematically motivated through connections to string theory anomaly cancellation, optimal spherical codes, and high-dimensional lattice packing. A modal-logic foundation is introduced via five axioms of existence formalized in Kripke-frame semantics, establishing relational ontology, structural compressibility, multi-scale persistence, energetic cost of erasure, and self-reference as the basis for consciousness.

While classical and discrete, the model offers a computationally tractable platform for exploring the intersection of supersymmetry, coding theory, and cosmological structure.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The quest for unified physical theories has repeatedly revealed deep connections between seemingly disparate mathematical structures. The ASH Model emerges from the observation that the number nine appears recurrently in contexts ranging from mythological cosmologies to modern theoretical physics and coding theory.

This work constructs a 9-dimensional hypercube $\mathcal{H}_9 = (\{0,1\}^9, E)$, where each vertex encodes a cosmological realm as a binary string of length nine. Adinkra graphs, originally developed to represent off-shell supersymmetric multiplets \citep{fauxgates2005}, are embedded to supply transformation rules that double as linear error-correcting codes.

\section{Related Work}
\subsection{Supersymmetry and Adinkras}
Adinkras are topological graphs that encode the algebraic structure of one-dimensional supersymmetric theories \citep{fauxgates2005, doran2007}. Their bipartite nature and edge coloring correspond to supersymmetry generators, while their chromatic properties link directly to error-correcting codes.
