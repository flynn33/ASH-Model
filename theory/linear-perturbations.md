# Linear Perturbations

Status: Draft

## Definitions

- Perturbation variable: a first-order deviation around a defined background solution.
- Gauge condition: a rule for separating physical perturbations from representation freedom.
- Perturbation observable: a statistic derived from the perturbation equations and bridge map.

## Assumptions

- Perturbation equations depend on a completed background model.
- Gauge redundancy must be stated before perturbation observables are compared to data.

## Theorem or model obligations

- Define perturbation variables and gauge conditions.
- Linearize the background equations or microscopic dynamics under stated assumptions.
- Derive stability criteria and mode evolution.
- Map perturbation solutions to observable statistics.

## Required tests

- Symbolic or numerical checks of the linearization.
- Stability tests for representative parameter settings.
- Matched-ablation tests after observable statistics are defined.

## Known gaps

- No background equations exist.
- No perturbation variables or gauge choices are selected.
- No perturbation-to-observable map is defined.
